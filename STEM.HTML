Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Strand

STEM Strand

The STEM (Science,Technology,Engineering, and Mathematics) refers to any subjects that fall under these four disciplines. Students in senior high school will participate in educational activities that will sharpen their knowledge and abilities in data analysis, real-world impacts, and research techniques.

Major Subject and Strand Focus:

GENERAL CHEMISTRY- Learning basic concepts including atomic structure, molecular structure, chemical bonding, and acids and bases are covered in a general chemistry course.

GENERAL PHYSICS- Using techniques and ideas from algebra, geometry, trigonometry, graphical analysis, and basic calculus, study of the mechanics of particles, rigid bodies, fluids, waves, heat, and thermodynamics.

Courses for STEM Students

Bachelor of Science in Engineering

BS in Engineering

This kind of engineering degree equips graduates with the knowledge and abilities to work as professionals in a wide range of sectors. Engineering bachelor's degree candidates frequently concentrate in fields like computer, electrical, mechanical, or industrial engineering

Bachelor of Science in Data Science

BS in Data Science

Data Science is a subfield of computer science that focuses on programming, analytics, and statistics. Building hardware and writing software are topics covered by computer science

Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics

BS in Applied Mathematics

Is a four-year program that gives students a solid foundation in various mathematical approaches and how they can be used to data analysis, optimization, and modeling. Arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and analysis are the study areas covered in this course

BS in Health Sciences / Life Sciences

BS in Health Sciences / Life Sciences

A student can study the human species and its body with this curriculum. This program offers a comprehensive education on how people live and interact with the environment.

Bachelor of Science in Architecture

BS in Architecture

Is a ten-semester pre-professional undergraduate degree that, by combining discipline coursework with continuing, real-world design experience, prepares students for the BAC's expedited two-year master of architecture program

Bachelor of Science in Applied Physics

BS in Applied Physics

Is the application of all physical theories to a wide range of real-world issues in engineering, research, and business. The pragmatic and abstract elements of physics are combined in this BS in Applied Physics curriculum

Bachelor of Science in Food Technology

BS in Food Technology

A four-year program that emphasizes the food's scientific preparation, processing, and delivery. The practice of turning raw resources into food comprises a variety of methods and procedures

BS in Biology / Biochemistry / Chemistry

BS in Biology / Biochemistry / Chemistry

This examines the molecular basis of life and the interface between biology and chemistry.
