Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW) Strand

SMAW Strand

The SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) strand focuses on teaching students the fundamental techniques and skills of arc welding, including electrode selection, welding positions, and proper welding procedures, to equip them with the knowledge and expertise required for a career in welding and metal fabrication.

Major Subject: Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) is a major subject that encompasses the practical application and theoretical understanding of welding using a manual arc welding process, where a consumable electrode coated in flux is used to create strong and durable metal joints. Students learn the essential skills and techniques required for various welding applications, ensuring they are well-prepared for careers in welding and related fields.

Activities of SMAW Students

The SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) strand at AASMNHS involves hands-on training and skill development in various welding techniques using shielded metal arc welding equipment. Students in the SMAW strand engage in practical welding exercises, learning how to join and fabricate metal structures and components using arc welding processes.

Courses for SMAW Students

Bachelor of Science in Welding Engineering

BS in Welding Engineering

This program combines engineering principles with welding technology, offering courses that cover various welding processes, including SMAW. Students learn about welding design, metallurgy, materials science, and welding process optimization.

Bachelor of Science in Welding Technology

BS in Welding Technology

This program provides a comprehensive education in welding techniques, including SMAW. Courses may cover welding fundamentals, welding codes and standards, welding inspection, and welding automation, with a focus on hands-on training and laboratory work.

Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering Technology

BS in Mechanical Engineering Technology

This program combines mechanical engineering principles with specialized training in welding technology. Students take courses that cover SMAW and other welding processes, along with courses in mechanical design, manufacturing processes, and materials science.

Bachelor of Science in Manufacturing Engineering Technology

BS in Manufacturing Engineering Technology

This program integrates manufacturing engineering principles with a focus on welding and joining technologies. Courses cover various welding processes, including SMAW, along with topics such as materials selection, welding automation, and quality control
