Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Strand

HUMSS Strand

HUMSS is short for humanities and social sciences. It is an academic strand covering various fields that deal with the exploration of the human condition using analytical, critical, and empirical ways – and thereby studying changes in society and human behavior

Major Subject: Creative Non Fiction (CNF)

Creative nonfiction is a genre of writing that uses elements of creative writing to present a factual, true story. Literary techniques that are usually reserved for writing fiction can be used in creative nonfiction, such as dialogue, scene-setting, and narrative arcs.

Outputs in HUMSS

Using varied mediums of human interaction, students in HUMSS likewise develop their communicative competence in the use of English and Filipino languages as they are expected to produce literary, research-based, performance-based, academic and digital outputs.

Courses for HUMSS Students

Bachelor of Science (BS) in Psychology

BS in Psychology

The BS in Psychology degree prepares you for general careers in teaching, research, counseling and human resources. It can also be a foundation for further studies in the fields of Medicine, Guidance and Counseling, Human Resource Development and Law.

Bachelor of Arts (AB) in Communication Arts

BA in Communication Arts

The Communication Arts Program in Production is designed to introduce the student to the media – particularly print, film, radio, photography and television.

Bachelor of Arts (BA) in Philosophy

BA in Philosophy

The program teaches students how to analyze philosophical arguments and texts, construct good arguments and present arguments in a logical way. It also has special emphasis on Philippine culture, arts, politics, and folkways.

Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSED)

BSED

The program combines both theory and practice in order to teach students the necessary knowledge and skills a high school teacher needs. The program aims to produce competent teachers who provide a conducive learning experience to their students.
